Full-Width Grid Kanban Wall

A clean-sheet wall zoning concept for the Grid office: make the back wall taller, run the Kanban mirror east-to-west, anchor TODO visibility in the War Room, reserve the northeast corner for Launch Control merges, and turn the southwest corner into a failure and timeout recovery area.

Selected Wall Direction

The mirror becomes an architectural wall, not a small board. It spans from the War Room side through northeast Launch Control and becomes the office's primary information plane.

Wall-first layout

Zoning Decisions

These are the design calls implied by the sketch. They are intentionally about room semantics first; exact blocker geometry can come later.

TODO anchors the War Room TODO should be visible over the War Room so unstarted work feels like planning pressure, not just a small lane on a board.
Center wall shows flow state In progress, review, and done can stretch across the workshop center and east side. The lanes should read as one continuous mirror, split by room geography.
Southwest becomes recovery Failed plays, timeouts, blocked agents, and hard error states should route to a red recovery corner instead of disappearing into the quiet Zen idle area.
Area New meaning Visual treatment
Back wall west / War Room Planning TODO, priority queue, unstarted work pressure. Tall yellow TODO lane, dense cards, visible from the left side of the office.
Back wall center Active work and review flow over the workshop. Cyan and violet lanes with dotted agent routes feeding workstations.
Back wall east / NE corner Launch Control for merge and ship decisions. Orange merge rail tied into the full-width mirror and NE room.
Southwest corner Error, timeout, blocked, and failed-play recovery area. Red diagnostic bay, warning cards, and parked failed agents distinct from Zen idle.
Zen Garden Only healthy idle/rest state. Keep green/calm and visually separate from failure recovery.
